About PPLV

WHAT IS THE PPLV GROUP?

Programming Principles, Logic and Verification consists of developing mathematical proofs to make computer systems more safe, reliable and secure. The group covers a very wide range of study, but you can think of it to be separated into three main groups:[1]

  • Programming Princicles - covers the study of programming languages in order to help programmers write trustworthy programs.
  • Logic - one of the core skills of the PPLV group used to carry out all their projects.
  • Verification - the PPLV group also looks at security for both programs and systems.

GOALS

The PPLV group aims to develop mathematically sound proofs that support computer science concepts which they have been doing successfully since the group's start in 2012[2], and can now continue do so for the next 6 years! Recently, the group received one of the biggest program grants ever for the 'Interface Reasoning for Interactive Systems' project - watch the video to learn more.

Professor David Pym talks about the group's new multi-million pound grant.

Other than that, the group strives to collaborate with teams in other areas such as artificial intelligence, security, biology, and economics; as well as work with more top companies, like NASA and Facebook, to impact the world in a positive and profound way.[1]
