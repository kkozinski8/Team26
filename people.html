Meet the Professors

  • [1]

    David Pym

    Professor of Information, Logic, and Security at UCL, Head of PPLV Group

    He is a logician, mathematician, and computer scientist. His research is mainly in logic including both pure logic and on developing logic-based methods as a mathematical modelling technology for reasoning about systems and behaviour, and in security which is about security policy and security economics.
    David Pym has broad experience of research, teaching, and management in leading universities and in industry. He also takes the role of editors for several academic publications.

  • [2]

    Byron Cook

    Professor of Computer Science at UCL and Director at Amazon Web Services

    Professor Byron Cook's interests include computer/network security, program analysis/verification, programming languages, theorem proving, logic, hardware design, operating systems, and biological systems. At Amazon Byron leads a research group focused on formal/constraint-based tools for reasoning about cloud-computing security.[2]

  • [3]

    Peter O'Hearn

    Professor of Computer Science at UCL and a Research Scientist at Facebook

    Professor Peter O'Hearn's research include theoretical topics like category-theoretic models and logics as well as the research on methods that automatically analyses softwares products developed by industries to find out possible flaws. He also believes that the theory, software tools and applications should be linked to other during research by mutual feedback.[3]

  • [4]

    Alexandra Silva

    Professor of Algebra, Semantics, and Computation at the PPLV Group, UCL

    Professor Alexandra Silva focuses her research on algorithms for models of computations and the modular development of specification languages . A major percentage of her work was developed from a mathematical framework called coalgebra, which was developed in recent years.[4]
