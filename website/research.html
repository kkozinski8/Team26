Research

Resource Reasoning

This is a four-year project starting from 01 March 2012 to 30 June 2016. Led by Professor D from University College London, it is categorised under fundamentals of computing and computer systems & architectures.

It’s main idea is about resource. Resource has always been a crucial aspect to be considered for all computer programmes and systems. Suitable and efficient usages of recourses such as memory, files, processor time must be ensured for healthy and sustainable working of systems. Thus it is natural for programmers to consider thoroughly about how the system deals with and allocates resource. The research team believes that there can be a new kind of mathematical theory, that is called resource reasoning, which connects closely with engineers' informal thinking about resources.

The research team believes that resource should be considered not only in pre-formal system design, but the formal analysis and verification of programs as well now and in future. Undoubtedly, it is difficult to develop a mature theory (or theories) that both engages with their informal hunches, and generates tractable, reliable analysis and verification techniques for many problem. The research aimed new theoretical facts and new prototypes which which could help to eliminate frequent crash, mishandling of data and to enhance reliability.

The team has collaborated with researchers from other institutions to release a number of valuable paper regarding this topic:

  • “Sub-Classical Boolean Bunched Logics and the Meaning of Par” By James Brotherston and Jules Villard
    • - “A decision procedure for satisfiability in separation logic with inductive predicates (2014)” By James Brotherston, Carsten Fuhs, Juan A. Navarro Perez and Nikos Gorogianni
  • “Sub-Classical Boolean Bunched Logics and the Meaning of Par” By Gabrielle Anderson and David Pym
  • “A calculus and logic of bunched resources and processes” By Gabrielle Anderson and David Pym

The results developed in this project are used for new projects in diverse areas and are applied in security policies in human-centred organizations. The researchers involved in this projects are now working for big companies like Amazon, Facebook and Google. They might fuse these ideas to their R&D to impact more areas.

Find out more from their official website: http://www.resourcereasoning.com/
